and even T0-Vakuum instability. Yet QFT never evolved into a theory of gravity, and the physics community
resorted instead to geometric General Relativity (GR), which remains incompatible with quantum theory.
This chapter explains in detail why QFT never became a T0-Vakuum-curvature theory, how historical biases
prevented scientists from interpreting the T0-Vakuum correctly, and how FFGFT completes the conceptual
unification that QFT mathematically hinted at for decades.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item QFT Already Contains FFGFT’s Mathematical Structure
\end{enumerate}
QFT expresses every complex field in the form:
Φ = ρ e^{i$\theta$},
where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item ρ = amplitude of the field,
  \item $\theta$ = phase of the field.
\end{itemize}
This decomposition is identical to the foundation of FFGFT. In FFGFT:
\begin{itemize}
  \item ρ becomes T0-Vakuum amplitude (origin of inertia, curvature, gravity, mass),
  \item $\theta$ becomes T0-Vakuum phase (origin of propagation, coherence, time).
\end{itemize}
Thus, the seeds of FFGFT were fully present in QFT formalism. What was missing was the interpretation:
the recognition that ρ and $\theta$ describe the physical T0-Vakuum, not just mathematical field components.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Why Physicists Rejected Physical T0-Vakuum Models
\end{enumerate}
After the failure of the 19th-century luminiferous aether, physicists became allergic to the idea of a
physical T0-Vakuum. Einstein’s formulation of relativity removed the need for a medium, and the scientific
community treated this as a philosophical victory.
This created an ideological barrier: "There must be no T0-Vakuum medium."

substrate.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The Fundamental Conceptual Error: Quantizing Geometry

\begin{itemize}
  \item string theory,
  \item loop quantum gravity,
  \item spin foams.
\end{itemize}
Every attempt failed because:
you cannot quantize geometry if geometry is not fundamental.
