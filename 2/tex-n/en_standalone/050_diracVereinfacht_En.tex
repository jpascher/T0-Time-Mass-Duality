	\section{The Fundamental Insight: Clifford Algebra}
	
	\subsection{The Problem with 4×4 Matrices}
	
	The standard Dirac equation is usually written as:
	\begin{equation}
		(i\gamma^\mu \partial_\mu - m)\psi = 0
	\end{equation}
	
	with complex 4×4 matrices $\gamma^\mu$.
	
	\textbf{The question:} Why 4×4 matrices? Are they fundamental?
	
	\textbf{The answer:} No. The matrices are a \textbf{representation}, not the 
	fundamental physics.
	
	\subsection{The Abstract Form}
	
	The fundamental Dirac equation is a Clifford algebra equation:
	\begin{equation}
		\boxed{(i \mathbf{e}_\mu \partial^\mu - m)\Psi = 0}
	\end{equation}
	
	where:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item $\mathbf{e}_\mu$: Abstract basis vectors of spacetime (not matrices!)
		\item $\Psi$: Geometric object in the spin bundle
		\item Clifford rule: $\mathbf{e}_\mu \mathbf{e}_\nu + \mathbf{e}_\nu \mathbf{e}_\mu = 2g_{\mu\nu}$
	\end{itemize}
	
	The 4×4 matrices $\gamma^\mu$ are only \textbf{one possible matrix representation} 
	of the abstract basis vectors $\mathbf{e}^\mu$.
	
	\begin{keypoint}[Representation vs. Physics]
		\textbf{Fundamental:} Clifford algebra structure \\
		\textbf{Representation:} 4×4 matrices (calculation tool)
		
		The matrices are \textbf{not} the physics, but a tool for calculation.
	\end{keypoint}
	
	\section{Spin as Topology}
	
	\subsection{The 720° Rotation}
	
	Spin-1/2 particles have the well-known property:
	\begin{equation}
		R(360°)\Psi = -\Psi \quad \text{and} \quad R(720°)\Psi = \Psi
	\end{equation}
	
	This is \textbf{not a matrix property}, but follows directly from the 
	Clifford algebra structure!
	
	\subsection{Winding Numbers on the Torus}
	
	In T0 theory, spin is interpreted geometrically:
	\begin{equation}
		\text{Spin-}s \longleftrightarrow \text{Winding } (n_\theta, n_\phi) 
		\text{ with } \frac{n_\phi}{n_\theta} = 2s

	\textbf{Spin-1/2:} Winding $(1, 1)$ on the torus \\
	The 720° rotation = traversing this winding twice
	
	This is \textbf{pure topology}, not a mysterious quantum property!
