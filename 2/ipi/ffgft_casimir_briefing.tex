\begin{abstract}
\noindent The Fundamental Fractal Geometric Field Theory (FFGFT) makes a quantitative, parameter-free prediction for the characteristic Casimir vacuum scale: $L_\xi \approx 100{,}24\,\mu$m. The prediction is derived from the cosmic microwave background (CMB) energy density and a single dimensionless constant $\xi = 4/30{,}000$. This note summarises the derivation, the consistency with the standard Casimir formula, and the embedding of $L_\xi$ in a five-step $\xi$-power ladder linking the Planck length to the Hubble length. Source documents in the FFGFT corpus: Doc~091 (Casimir-CMB), Doc~165 (Hubble), Doc~166 (synthesis).
\end{abstract}

\section{Setting}

FFGFT operates with a single dimensionless parameter,
\[
\xi = \frac{4}{30{,}000} = 1{,}333 \times 10^{-4},
\]
and the scale-anchor relation $\tilde T \cdot m = 1$. From these and standard physical constants, FFGFT derives a hierarchy of physical length scales without further input parameters. Two of these scales are directly relevant for the Casimir effect: a sub-Planck saturation length $L_0 = \xi \cdot \ell_P$ and a vacuum-emergent length $L_\xi$ linked to the CMB.

\section{The CMB-Casimir Relation}

The central relation in Doc~091 reads:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\mathrm{CMB}} = \frac{\xi \, \hbar c}{L_\xi^4}\,.
\label{eq:cmb_casimir}
\end{equation}
With the measured CMB energy density $\rho_{\mathrm{CMB}} = 4{,}17 \times 10^{-14}$ J/m$^3$ and $\xi = 4/30{,}000$, the relation fixes
\begin{equation}
L_\xi = \left(\frac{\xi\,\hbar c}{\rho_{\mathrm{CMB}}}\right)^{1/4} \approx 100{,}24\,\mu\text{m}.
\label{eq:Lxi_value}
\end{equation}
Equivalently, using the Stefan-Boltzmann form $\rho_{\mathrm{CMB}} = (\pi^2/15)(k_B T_{\mathrm{CMB}})^4 / (\hbar c)^3$:
\begin{equation}
L_\xi = \left(\frac{15\,\xi}{\pi^2}\right)^{1/4} \frac{\hbar c}{k_B T_{\mathrm{CMB}}} = 100{,}24\,\mu\text{m}\,,
\label{eq:Lxi_from_T}
\end{equation}
which agrees with the direct evaluation to 0.11\%.

The corresponding characteristic frequency is
\[
f_\xi = \frac{c}{L_\xi} \approx 2{,}99\,\text{THz},
\]
placing $L_\xi$ in the same logarithmic regime as the Casimir target frequencies discussed in the IPI correspondence (Moseley's $\nu_M \approx 8{,}23$~THz / $\lambda_M \approx 36{,}4\,\mu$m), with a separation of factor $\sim 2{,}7$.

\section{Consistency with the Standard Casimir Formula}

The modified Casimir energy density in FFGFT is
\begin{equation}
|\rho_{\mathrm{Casimir}}(d)| = \frac{\pi^2}{240\,\xi}\,\rho_{\mathrm{CMB}}\left(\frac{L_\xi}{d}\right)^4\,.
\label{eq:modified_casimir}
\end{equation}
Substituting Eq.~\eqref{eq:cmb_casimir} into Eq.~\eqref{eq:modified_casimir} yields
\begin{equation}
|\rho_{\mathrm{Casimir}}(d)| = \frac{\pi^2}{240\,\xi}\cdot\frac{\xi\,\hbar c}{L_\xi^4}\cdot\frac{L_\xi^4}{d^4} = \frac{\pi^2 \hbar c}{240\,d^4}\,,
\end{equation}
exactly the standard Casimir formula. The parameter $\xi$ cancels at the operational level. The FFGFT formulation is therefore consistent with all current Casimir measurements that confirm the standard $1/d^4$ scaling, while reframing the absolute energy scale through $L_\xi$ and $\rho_{\mathrm{CMB}}$.

\section{The Casimir-Hubble Bridge Relation}

FFGFT also predicts an algebraic relation between the Casimir scale and the Hubble constant. Combining Eq.~\eqref{eq:Lxi_from_T} with the FFGFT prediction for $H_0$ in Doc~165, $\hbar H_0 / (m_e c^2) = (\pi/2)\,\xi^{10}$, gives:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;L_\xi \cdot \frac{H_0}{c} = \frac{\pi}{2}\left(\frac{15}{\pi^2}\right)^{1/4} \frac{m_e c^2}{k_B T_{\mathrm{CMB}}}\,\xi^{41/4}\;}
\label{eq:bridge}
\end{equation}
The exponent decomposes as
\[
\frac{41}{4} = \underbrace{10}_{\text{H}_0\text{ exponent (Doc 165)}} + \underbrace{\frac{1}{4}}_{\text{Casimir exponent}}\,,
\]
which is the same exponent appearing in Doc~026 as $E_H = E_0 \cdot \xi^{41/4}$. Numerically, both sides of Eq.~\eqref{eq:bridge} agree to $10^{-14}\%$. The relation contains no free parameters; all quantities are either physical constants or the single $\xi$.

In numerical form:
\begin{equation}
L_\xi \cdot \frac{H_0}{c} = 7{,}241 \times 10^{-31}\,, \qquad \frac{m_e c^2}{k_B T_{\mathrm{CMB}}} = 2{,}000 \times 10^9\,.
\end{equation}

\section{Summary of Predictions}

\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
\item $L_\xi = 100{,}24\,\mu$m \emph{follows} from $\rho_{\mathrm{CMB}}$ and a single dimensionless $\xi = 4/30{,}000$. No free fit parameters.
\item Substitution into the modified Casimir density Eq.~\eqref{eq:modified_casimir} reproduces the standard $\pi^2 \hbar c/(240\,d^4)$ exactly. Mathematical consistency with all standard Casimir measurements at the operational level is preserved.
\item $L_\xi$ sits on a five-step $\xi$-power ladder spanning Planck to Hubble scale; it is approximately $2{,}12\times$ the geometric mean $\sqrt{\ell_P \cdot c/H_0}$.
\item The Casimir scale and the Hubble constant satisfy the algebraic bridge Eq.~\eqref{eq:bridge}, with combined $\xi$-exponent $41/4 = 10 + 1/4$.
\item The corresponding frequency $c/L_\xi \approx 3$ THz lies in the same regime as Casimir-scale phenomenology discussed elsewhere in the IPI correspondence.
\end{enumerate}
