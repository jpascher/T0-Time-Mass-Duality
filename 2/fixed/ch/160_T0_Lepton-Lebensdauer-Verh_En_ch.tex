	\section{Starting Point}
	
	The muon ($\mu$) and the tau lepton ($\tau$) decay via the weak interaction. Their lifetimes are experimentally known to high precision:
	\begin{align}
		\tau_\mu &= 2.1969 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{s} \\
		\tau_\tau &= 2.903 \times 10^{-13} \, \text{s}
	\end{align}
	
	The ratio is:
	\begin{equation}
		\frac{\tau_\mu}{\tau_\tau} = 7{,}567{,}689
	\end{equation}
	
	In the Standard Model, this ratio is calculated from Fermi theory. The decay width of a lepton $\ell$ scales as $\Gamma_\ell \propto G_F^2 m_\ell^5$, modified by phase space corrections and the branching ratios of the various decay channels. The calculation is exact but requires numerous experimental input parameters.
	
	The goal of this work is to derive the same ratio from T0 parameters -- with minimal inputs and maximum geometric motivation.

	where $f_{\tau \to e}$ and $f_{\mu \to e}$ are the phase space corrections and $BR(\tau \to e \nu \bar\nu)$ is the branching ratio of the electronic tau decay.

		f_{\mu \to e} &= 0.999813 \\

	\textbf{3. Branching ratio:} This is the key -- and this is where T0 enters.

	The tau can decay into three types of channels:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item Electronic: $\tau \to e + \nu_\tau + \bar\nu_e$ (rate $\Gamma_e$)
		\item Muonic: $\tau \to \mu + \nu_\tau + \bar\nu_\mu$ (rate $\Gamma_\mu$)
		\item Hadronic: $\tau \to \text{hadrons} + \nu_\tau$ (rate $\Gamma_\text{had}$)
	\end{itemize}
	
	The ratio of the hadronic to the electronic rate defines $R_\text{had}$:
	\begin{equation}

	\begin{equation}
		BR(\tau \to e) = \frac{1}{1 + \dfrac{f_{\tau \to \mu}}{f_{\tau \to e}} + R_\text{had}}
