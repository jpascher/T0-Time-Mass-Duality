\section{\texorpdfstring{Correction~1: Prefactor in the Higgs Derivation of $\xi$}{Correction~1: Prefactor in the Higgs Derivation of}}
% ============================================================

\subsection*{Affected document}

Doc.~049 (T0 Lagrangian and Higgs integration), HTML intermediate version
and older working versions.

\subsection*{Erroneous expression}

\begin{equation}
  \xi \;=\; \frac{\lambda_h^2\, v^2}{64\pi^4\, m_h^2}
  \;\approx\; 1{.}0 \times 10^{-5}
  \tag{old -- incorrect}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Correct expression}

\begin{equation}
  \boxed{
    \xi \;=\; \frac{\lambda_h^2\, v^2}{16\pi^3\, m_h^2}
    \;\approx\; 1{.}33 \times 10^{-4}
  }
  \tag{190.1}
\end{equation}

with the experimental inputs:
\begin{align*}
  m_h      &= 125{.}25\,\text{GeV} \quad \text{(Higgs mass)}, \\
  v        &= 246{.}22\,\text{GeV} \quad \text{(vacuum expectation value)}, \\
  \lambda_h &= \frac{m_h^2}{2v^2} \approx 0{.}129
               \quad \text{(Higgs self-coupling)}.
\end{align*}

\subsection*{Justification}

The prefactor $64\pi^4$ originated from a typographical error in the early
HTML visualisation. It yields $\xi \approx 10^{-5}$, which deviates by one
order of magnitude from the geometrically derived value
$\xi = 4/3 \cdot 10^{-4}$ and does not reproduce the natural constants.
The correct prefactor $16\pi^3$ gives
$\xi \approx 1{.}33 \times 10^{-4}$, consistent with the independently
determined value from the proton-to-electron mass ratio (Doc.~009).

The individual T0 masses agree with experiment to $\sim 1\,\%$;
the high precision of $Q = 2/3$ arises from the specific
algebraic structure of the Koide combination, not from individual
mass accuracy.

\section{Clarification~5: Scope and Claim of the Mass Formulas}
% ============================================================

\subsection*{Context}

Several FFGFT documents describe mass derivations for leptons
and other particles. The scope of these derivations must be
communicated clearly.

\subsection*{Binding formulation}

The FFGFT mass formulas (e.g.\ $m_\ell = r_\ell \cdot \xi^{p_\ell} \cdot v$)
yield values that agree with experiment to $\sim 1\,\%$.
This is \textbf{not a precision calculation}, but a structural proof:

\begin{itemize}
  \item The mass formulas \emph{follow geometrically} from $\xi$ and $v$
    -- they are not fitted.
  \item The remaining $\sim 1\,\%$ deviations reflect
    measurement uncertainties and approximations, not structural errors.
  \item Numerically better calculations than the Standard Model are
    \emph{not} claimed.
\end{itemize}

In external texts, avoid: ``FFGFT calculates the masses exactly.''
Correct: ``FFGFT \emph{derives} the mass structure from a single
parameter $\xi$ and reproduces the orders of magnitude to $\sim 1\,\%$.''

\section{Clarification~6: Koide Formula Only with Original Values}
% ============================================================

\subsection*{Binding rule}

The Koide relation
\begin{equation}
  Q = \frac{m_e + m_\mu + m_\tau}{(\sqrt{m_e}+\sqrt{m_\mu}+\sqrt{m_\tau})^2} = \frac{2}{3}
  \tag{190.11}
\end{equation}
may only be evaluated with experimentally measured masses (PDG values).

\textbf{Not permitted:} inserting the symbolic FFGFT terms
$r_\ell \cdot \xi^{p_\ell} \cdot v$ directly into the formula.
The $\sqrt{m_i}$ operation generates cross-terms with mixed $\xi$-powers
that are foreign to the torus structure space.
Any such substitution gives $Q \approx 0{.}6677 \neq 2/3$ and is not
a contradiction of the theory but a category error.

\textbf{Permitted:} evaluating FFGFT terms numerically
(e.g.\ $m_\tau = 1783\,\text{MeV}$) and using that result
like a measured value in the Koide formula.

\textbf{Mathematical background:} The $r_\ell$ terms are
geometric invariants of their torus modes. The set of admissible
pairs $(r_i, p_i)$ is not closed under multiplication:
$r_e \cdot r_\mu = 64/15$ belongs to no torus mode.
This is the non-closure theorem (Doc.~193).
The general analysis of composition limits is in Doc.~192.
