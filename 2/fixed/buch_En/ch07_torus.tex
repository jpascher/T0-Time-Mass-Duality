\section{Infinite and Bounded --- Without Singularity}

The decisive topological property of the torus resolves several of the most persistent problems of physics at once.

The torus has \emph{finite volume} but \emph{no boundaries}. One can wander infinitely far on its surface without ever reaching a border. The surface is closed --- but topology alone does not exclude singularities. What is decisive is the combination of the compact topology with the \emph{mediator mass~$m_T$} of the torsion field, which acts as a natural regulator and prevents collapse to point singularities.

The consequences of this combination are dramatic:

For \textbf{black holes} this means: no point singularity. Instead, stable vacuum cores --- so-called T0 knots --- that stabilise the toroidal structure (cf.\ \href{https://github.com/jpascher/T0-Time-Mass-Duality/blob/main/2/pdf/149_FFGFT-torsion_De.pdf}{Doc.~149}). The collapse to a point of infinite density --- the spectre of general relativity --- does not occur. The mediator mass~$m_T$ of the torsion field acts as a natural brake.

For the \textbf{Big Bang} this means: no singular initial point. The universe is static and eternal. There was no moment of creation in a physical sense --- the universe \emph{is}, as timeless geometry.

For \textbf{field theory} this means: propagators never diverge. The natural regularisation provided by the torus makes the elaborate renormalisation techniques of standard QFT unnecessary. The infinities that tormented generations of physicists simply do not exist --- they were artefacts of a false assumption about the topology of spacetime.

The singularity theorems of Penrose and Hawking --- which prove that singularities in GR are \emph{unavoidable} --- are thereby resolved. The theorems remain valid, but their premises (in particular the energy conditions in non-compact spacetime) are modified in T0: the mediator mass~$m_T$ changes the effective energy condition near collapse, while the compact topology regularises the global structure.

An analogy may help: the surface of the Earth is finite but without boundary. One never falls ``off the edge of the world.'' Similarly, the universe in the T0 picture is finite in volume but without borders --- and one never encounters a singularity.
