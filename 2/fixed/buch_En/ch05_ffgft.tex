Two components that together carry all of physics:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\rho(x)$ is the \textbf{vacuum amplitude}. It is proportional to the local mass density: $\rho(x) \propto \mfield = 1/\Tfield$. Where $\rho$ is high, mass concentrates. Where $\rho$ is low, the vacuum is ``thinner'' --- and time passes faster.
\item $\theta(x)$ is the \textbf{vacuum phase}. It describes the rotational dynamics on the fundamental torus and encodes how local time evolves: $\dot{\theta} = m = 1/T$.
\end{itemize}

\section{The Four Pillars}

From this single field~$\Phi$ arise the four fundamental aspects of physics:

\subsection{Gravity Is Vacuum Convergence}

Gravity in FFGFT is not a separate force. It is not curvature of spacetime in Einstein's sense. It is \emph{coherent phase curvature} of the vacuum field. Where the field $\Phi$ converges --- where $\rho$ increases --- what we experience as gravitational attraction arises. Not because a force acts, but because the geometry of the vacuum itself flows towards that point.

\subsection{Quantum Mechanics Is Vacuum Coherence}

The wave functions of quantum mechanics are modes of the vacuum field. Interference, superposition, entanglement --- all these seemingly mysterious phenomena are properties of $\Phi$. A particle in superposition means: the vacuum field has multiple coherent modes at that location. When coherence is lost (decoherence), superposition collapses --- not through a mysterious ``collapse'' but through the loss of fractal coherence between subsystems (cf.\ Chapter~\ref{ch:determinismus} and~\ref{ch:bewusstsein}).

\subsection{Mass Is Vacuum Energy}

Mass is not ``added'' to a field --- through a Higgs mechanism or any other external process. Mass \emph{is} the local amplitude of the vacuum field. An electron is a stable resonance in $\Phi$, a proton another, more complex resonance. The mass is the frequency of this resonance.

\subsection{Black Holes Are Stable Vacuum Cores}

And here FFGFT resolves one of the most persistent problems of theoretical physics: the singularity problem. In general relativity, matter beyond the Schwarzschild radius collapses to a point of infinite density --- a singularity. In FFGFT this does not happen. The mediator mass $m_T$ of the torsion field prevents the collapse. Black holes are not points but stable, high-density vacuum cores --- T0 knots that stabilise the toroidal structure of spacetime.

\section{The Lagrangian}

The dynamics of the field $\Phi$ are described by a Lagrangian derivable from time-mass duality:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_0 = \varepsilon (\partial \Delta m)^2 \qquad \text{with } \varepsilon \propto \xsi^4/\lambda^2
\end{equation}

The mapping to the DVFT formulation is direct:
\begin{equation}
(\partial \Delta m)^2 \;\longrightarrow\; (\partial \rho)^2 + \rho^2 (\partial \theta)^2
\end{equation}

The first term describes the dynamics of the mass distribution, the second the phase dynamics. Together they yield the complete field equation of the universe.
