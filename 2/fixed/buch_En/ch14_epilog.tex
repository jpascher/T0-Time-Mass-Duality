In T0 everything can be traced back to one of three dual concepts: everything is energy ($E$ as the fundamental field). Or: everything is mass ($m = E/c^2 = 1/T$). Or: everything is time ($T = 1/m$).

The choice is free. If one chooses energy as fundamental, mass and time do not exist as independent quantities --- they are derived shadows. If one chooses time, mass and energy become frequencies. If one chooses mass, time and energy are reciprocals and products.

There is no ``correct'' foundation. The duality~$T \cdot m = 1$ says: both sides are on equal footing. The universe has no privileged mode of description.

\section{God Stands Outside the System}

If the entire universe is a torus --- infinite, bounded, singularity-free --- then a possible creator stands \emph{outside} this system. Not outside time, for time is merely a phase coordinate on the torus. Not outside space, for space is the torus structure itself. But outside the \emph{entire mathematical construct}.

The theory describes the \emph{how} with remarkable precision. It does not describe the \emph{why} --- why there is something rather than nothing. This question lies beyond the reach of any physical theory, including this one.

\section{Our Constructs Are Not Reality}

The equations --- $T \cdot m = 1$, $\Phi = \rho \cdot e^{i\theta}$, $\xsi = 4/30000$ --- are \emph{maps}, not the territory. We never grasp reality absolutely.

Every unit system --- SI, natural, T0 --- is a convention. Every formula is an approximation; even $\Kfrak = 0.9867$ is a linearisation. The fractal dimension~$\Df \approx 2.9999$ is an approximation (cf.\ Chapter~\ref{ch:fraktal}) to something that may not even be a number in the usual sense.

What the theory achieves: it shows that nature can be described with a single parameter --- a breathtaking simplification. What it does not achieve: explaining why there is something rather than nothing.

Mathematics is the most powerful tool we have. But tools are not reality itself.

\begin{kerngedanke}
The deeper we look, the simpler the structure becomes --- but the last layer remains hidden from us. Perhaps it must be so. Humility before nature is not a weakness of a theory but a sign of its maturity.
\end{kerngedanke}
