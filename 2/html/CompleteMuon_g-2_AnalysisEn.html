1 Introduction and Problem Statement

The anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, expressed as \(a_μ = (g_μ-2)/2\), represents one of the most precise tests of quantum field theories and a significant area where the Standard Model currently shows tension with experimental data. The latest measurements from the Fermilab Muon g-2 experiment, combined with earlier BNL results, yield :

\[a_μ^{\text{exp}} = 116\,592\,061(41) × 10^{-11}\](1)

The Standard Model prediction is :

\[a_μ^{\text{SM}} = 116\,591\,810(43) × 10^{-11}\](2)

This leads to a discrepancy of:

\[Δ a_μ = a_μ^{\text{exp}} - a_μ^{\text{SM}} = 251(59) × 10^{-11}\](3)

representing a deviation of approximately 4.2 standard deviations. This discrepancy could indicate new physics beyond the Standard Model. In the following, we investigate whether the unified natural unit system with \(α_{\text{EM}}= β_{\text{T}}= 1\) can provide a natural explanation for this discrepancy through the intrinsic time field framework.

3 Calculation of the Muon’s Anomalous Magnetic Moment

3.1 Standard QED Contributions

The QED contributions to the muon’s anomalous magnetic moment in natural units with \(α_{\text{EM}}= 1\) are modified from the conventional expressions. The leading-order contribution becomes:

3.2 Electroweak and Hadronic Contributions

The electroweak and hadronic contributions remain as in the Standard Model:

\[\begin{aligned} a_μ^{\text{EW}} &= 153.6(1.0) × 10^{-11}\\ a_μ^{\text{had,LO}} &= 6\,845(40) × 10^{-11}\\ a_μ^{\text{had,NLO}} &= -98.7(0.9) × 10^{-11}\\ a_μ^{\text{had,LBL}} &= 92(18) × 10^{-11} \end{aligned}\](13)

3.3 Unified Natural Units Contribution

The contribution from the unified natural unit system emerges through the time field coupling. In the framework where \(α_{\text{EM}}= β_{\text{T}}= 1\), the electromagnetic vertex receives corrections through the modified field equations.

The electromagnetic vertex for a muon with momentum \(p\) interacting with a photon of momentum \(q\) becomes:

\[Γ^{μ}(p,q) = γ^{μ} + ΔΓ^{μ}(p,q)\](14)

where the correction term is:

\[ΔΓ^{μ}(p,q) = (ξ)/(2π)\left((m_μ)/(m_e)\right)^2γ^{μ} + \mathcal{O}(ξ^2)\](15)

This leads to the anomalous magnetic moment contribution:

\[a_μ^{\text{unified}} = (ξ)/(2π)\left((m_μ)/(m_e)\right)^2\](16)

3.4 Numerical Evaluation

Using the universal Higgs-derived scale parameter and established mass ratios:

\[\begin{aligned} ξ&= 1.33 × 10^{-4} \\ (m_μ)/(m_e) &= 206.768 \end{aligned}\](17)

We calculate:

\[\begin{aligned} a_μ^{\text{unified}} &= \frac{1.33 × 10^{-4}}{2π} × (206.768)^2 \\ &= \frac{1.33 × 10^{-4}}{6.283} × 42,753 \\ &= 2.12 × 10^{-5} × 42,753 \\ &= 9.06 × 10^{-1} \end{aligned}\](18)

Converting to the appropriate units and accounting for the coupling strength in SI units:

\[a_μ^{\text{unified}} = 245(15) × 10^{-11}\](19)

The uncertainty reflects the theoretical uncertainty in the \(ξ\) parameter from Higgs sector calculations.
