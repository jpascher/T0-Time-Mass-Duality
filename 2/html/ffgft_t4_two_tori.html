What you are looking at

In Fundamental Fractal Geometric Field Theory (FFGFT), the foundational geometric object is not ordinary 3D space but a 4-dimensional torus T⁴. This is the Cartesian product of two independent 2-tori: T⁴ = T²(θ₁, θ₂) × T²(θ₃, θ₄). Each T² carries two independent angular coordinates running from 0 to 2π.

The two tori shown here are not separate objects — they are two projections of a single 4-dimensional structure. A particle trajectory on T⁴ winds simultaneously around both tori. The winding numbers p and q on each torus encode the quantum numbers of the particle — different combinations correspond to different particle generations.

Color encodes θ₄, the fourth angular coordinate. Since it cannot be shown as a spatial dimension, it is mapped onto the color spectrum (blue → green → yellow → red → violet → blue = one full 2π cycle). Points of equal color on both tori share the same θ₄ value — they are simultaneous in the fourth dimension. The color itself is the coupling between the two tori: no connecting line is needed, because equal color means equal phase, not a bridge between two separate objects.

The central relation of FFGFT is T̃ · m = 1, where T̃ is the intrinsic time scale and m is mass. Time is not an independent background dimension but is geometrically coupled to mass through the single dimensionless parameter ξ = 4/30000, from which all Standard Model constants are derived.

Try this: Set the same p:q ratio on both tori — the winding structures resonate, representing the same particle. Change one ratio — the structures diverge, representing a different particle generation. The color coupling (θ₄) remains unchanged regardless of the winding numbers.
