Overview of Publications on Time-Mass Duality

A Theoretical Framework for Extending Modern Physics

This overview presents a collection of works developing a new theoretical framework to extend physics: the Time-Mass Duality. This approach proposes a rethinking of time and mass, offering solutions to unresolved questions in quantum mechanics, quantum field theory, and cosmology—such as nonlocality or dark energy. The documents form a program spanning foundational ideas, mathematical models, and practical applications, with recent additions enhancing its cosmological and measurement perspectives.

Starting Point: Field Theory as the Initial Idea

Field Theory and Quantum Correlations

The starting point, questioning instant particle connections (nonlocality) and proposing a new field structure.

Conceptual Foundations and Motivation

The Necessity of Extending Standard Quantum Mechanics and Quantum Field Theory

Highlights weaknesses in conventional theories and introduces Time-Mass Duality as a solution.

Summary - Complementary Dualism in Physics - From Wave-Particle to Time-Mass Concept

Simply explains Time-Mass Duality as a complement to wave-particle duality.

A New Perspective on Time and Space: Johann Pascher's Revolutionary Ideas

Accessible to all, introducing the T₀ model with fixed time and variable mass.

Applications and Extensions

Explores whether light (photons) has variable mass and how this explains particle connections.

A Mathematical Analysis of Energy Dynamics

Applies the theory to the universe, viewing dark energy as distributing energy.

Cosmological and Frontier Areas

Investigates how the theory might address the smallest and biggest questions in physics.

Additional Relevant Documents

Shows how variable mass affects galaxies, explaining star movements without dark matter.

Unification of the T0 Model: Foundations - Dark Energy and Galaxy Dynamics

A comprehensive work applying the theory to cosmology and galaxies.

Natural Units with Fine-Structure Constant alpha = 1

Proposes a simple system where the fine-structure constant is set to 1.

Summary and Outlook

These works, now expanded with new documents on measurement differences and temperature adjustments, form a program rethinking physics. Time-Mass Duality uses simple rules to address big questions like nonlocality, dark energy, and cosmological observations. Future steps could include experimental testing, further model refinement, and simulations to create a unified physics framework.

Ein theoretischer Rahmen für die Erweiterung der modernen Physik

Diese Übersicht präsentiert eine Sammlung von Arbeiten, die einen neuen theoretischen Rahmen zur Erweiterung der Physik entwickeln: die Zeit-Masse-Dualität. Dieser Ansatz schlägt vor, Zeit und Masse neu zu betrachten, und bietet Lösungen für offene Fragen in der Quantenmechanik, Quantenfeldtheorie und Kosmologie – etwa zur Nichtlokalität oder dunklen Energie. Die Dokumente bilden ein Programm, das von Grundideen über mathematische Modelle bis zu praktischen Anwendungen reicht, ergänzt durch neue Arbeiten zu Messdifferenzen und Temperatureinheiten.

Zusammenfassung und Ausblick

Diese Arbeiten, nun erweitert durch neue Dokumente zu Messdifferenzen und Temperatureinheiten, bilden ein Programm, das die Physik neu denkt. Zukünftige Schritte könnten Tests der Theorie, genauere Modelle und Simulationen umfassen, um eine einheitliche Physik zu schaffen.
